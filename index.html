Bad Network Mode

When the internet is not working well - maybe due to a disaster - follow these steps for better performance on your iPhone and iPad, Mac or Android device.

iPhone and iPad

Step 1: Turn on Low Data Mode for Cellular

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Tap Cellular.
  3. Tap Cellular Data Options.
  4. If you see Data Mode, tap it.
  5. Select Low Data Mode, or turn on Low Data Mode if it appears as a switch.

Step 2: Turn on Low Data Mode for Wi-Fi

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Tap Wi-Fi
  3. Tap the Info button to the right of the Wi-Fi network that you're connected to.
  4. Turn on Low Data Mode.
Note that if you change Wi-Fi networks, you'll have to do this Step again.

Step 3: Turn off Background App Refresh

  1. Go back to Settings.
  2. Tap General.
  3. Tap Background App Refresh.
  4. Tap Background App Refresh again.
  5. Select Off.

Step 4: Turn off automatic app updates

  1. Go back to Settings.
  2. Scroll down and click the Apps icon.
  3. Tap App Store.
  4. Turn off App Updates.
  5. Under Cellular Data, turn off Automatic Downloads.

Step 5: Stop photo and cloud backup

  1. Go back to Settings.
  2. Tap your name at the top.
  3. Tap iCloud.
  4. Tap Photos.
  5. Turn off Sync this iPhone.

Step 6: Lower video quality when watching

  1. While watching a video in Youtube or wherever, tap the settings or gear icon.
  2. Select a lower option such as 480p or Data Saver.

Mac

Turn on Low Data Mode for Wi-Fi

  1. Open System Settings.
  2. Click Wi-Fi.
  3. Click the grey box labeled Details next to the Wi-Fi network that you're connected to.
  4. Turn on Low Data Mode.
Note that if you change Wi-Fi networks, you'll have to do this Step again.

Android

Step 1: Turn on Data Saver

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Tap Network and Internet.
  3. Tap Data Saver.
  4. Turn on Use Data Saver.

Step 2: Restrict background data

  1. Go back to Settings.
  2. Tap Apps.
  3. Tap See all apps.
  4. Select a high-usage app such as Facebook, Instagram, or YouTube.
  5. Tap Mobile data and Wi-Fi.
  6. Turn off Background data.
  7. Repeat for other nonessential apps.

Step 3: Turn off automatic app updates

  1. Open the Play Store.
  2. Tap your profile icon.
  3. Tap Settings.
  4. Tap Network preferences.
  5. Tap Auto-update apps.
  6. Select Don’t auto-update apps.

Step 4: Stop photo and cloud backup

  1. Open Google Photos.
  2. Tap your profile icon.
  3. Tap Photos settings.
  4. Tap Backup.
  5. Turn off Backup.

Step 5: Lower video quality when watching

  1. While watching a video in Youtube or wherever, tap the settings or gear icon.
  2. Select a lower quality such as 480p or Data Saver.

Done

Your phone will now use less data, avoid large downloads, and perform better on weak or congested networks.
