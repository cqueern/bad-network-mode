Bad Network Mode

When the internet is not working well after a disaster, follow these steps for better performance.

iPhone and iPad

Step 1: Turn on Low Data Mode

Open Settings.

Tap Cellular Data Options.

If you see Data Mode, tap it.

Select Low Data Mode, or turn on Low Data Mode if it appears as a switch.

Step 2: Turn off Background App Refresh

Go back to Settings.

Tap General.

Tap Background App Refresh.

Tap Background App Refresh again.

Select Off.

Step 3: Turn off automatic app updates

Go back to Settings.

Tap App Store.

Turn off App Updates.

Turn off Automatic Downloads.

Step 4: Stop photo and cloud backup

Go back to Settings.

Tap your name at the top.

Tap iCloud.

Tap Photos.

Turn off Sync this iPhone.

Step 5: Lower video quality when watching

When using video apps, look for Quality settings.

Select a lower option such as 480p or Data Saver.

Android

Step 1: Turn on Data Saver

Open Settings.

Tap Network and Internet.

Tap Data Saver.

Turn on Use Data Saver.

Step 2: Restrict background data

Go back to Settings.

Tap Apps.

Tap See all apps.

Select a high-usage app such as Facebook, Instagram, or YouTube.

Tap Mobile data and Wi-Fi.

Turn off Background data.

Repeat for other nonessential apps.

Step 3: Turn off automatic app updates

Open the Play Store.

Tap your profile icon.

Tap Settings.

Tap Network preferences.

Tap Auto-update apps.

Select Don’t auto-update apps.

Step 4: Stop photo and cloud backup

Open Google Photos.

Tap your profile icon.

Tap Photos settings.

Tap Backup.

Turn off Backup.

Step 5: Lower video quality when watching

When using video apps, open settings or the gear icon.

Select a lower quality such as 480p or Data Saver.

Done

Your phone will now use less data, avoid large downloads, and perform better on weak or congested networks.
